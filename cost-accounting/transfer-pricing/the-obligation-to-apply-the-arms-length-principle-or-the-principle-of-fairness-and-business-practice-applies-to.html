TRANSFER PRICING

The obligation to apply the Arm’s Length Principle or the Principle of Fairness and Business Practice applies to:

(A) Taxpayers who carry out affiliated transactions are required to prepare Transfer Pricing Documents based on PMK-213/PMK.03/2016

(B) ** Taxpayers who carry out affiliated transactions both required and not required to make Transfer Pricing Documents based on PMK-213/PMK.03/2016

(C) Taxpayers who carry out affiliated transactions that are not required to make Transfer Pricing Documents based on PMK-213/PMK.03/2016

(D) Taxpayers who carry out independent transactions based on PMK-213/PMK.03/2016

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The basis of transfer pricing is the Arm’s Length Principle, as it is known internationally. This principle states that the price agreed in a transaction between two related parties must be the same as the price agreed in a comparable transaction between two unrelated parties.

Concept note-2: -The arm’s length principle is a way of ensuring that organizations with multiple legal entities in different jurisdictions are tax-compliant and do not profit unfairly (accidentally or intentionally) from transacting between its related entities.

Concept note-3: -At the foundation of transfer pricing is the arm’s length principle, which states that the price charged in a controlled transaction between two related parties should be the same as that in a transaction between two unrelated parties on the open market.

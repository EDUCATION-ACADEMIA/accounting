What is the independence threshold used in Greece?

(A) 10%

(B) 25%

(C) 30%

(D) ** 33%

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -In 1832, a Treaty of Constantinople acknowledged Greece as an independent nation. The Greek War of Independence (from the year 1821â€“to the year 1829), as well known colloquially as the Greek Revolution, was a successful war fought by Greeks to gain independence from the Ottoman Empire.

Concept note-2: -Greece was part of the Ottoman Empire from the 15th century. Its struggle for independence began in 1821. In 1832, the Treaty of Constantinople recognised Greece as an independent nation.

Concept note-3: -Independence was finally granted by the Treaty of Constantinople in July 1832 when Greece (Hellas) was recognized as a free country. The Greeks were the first of the subject peoples of the Ottoman Empire to secure recognition as a sovereign power. Greeks celebrate their independence day annually on March 25.

What is the size of the comparison between debt and capital according to (DER) PMK 169/2015 and Per 25/2017 that applies:

(A) 1:1

(B) 2:1

(C) 3:1

(D) ** 4:1

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The debt-to-equity ratio tells a company the amount of risk associated with the way its capital structure is set up and run. The ratio highlights the amount of debt a company is using to run their business and the financial leverage that is available to a company.

Concept note-2: -A D/E ratio of 2 indicates that the company derives two-thirds of its capital financing from debt and one-third from shareholder equity, so it borrows twice as much funding as it owns (2 debt units for every 1 equity unit).

Concept note-3: -The Formula for Debt-To-Capital Ratio The debt-to-capital ratio is calculated by dividing a company’s total debt by its total capital, which is total debt plus total shareholders’ equity.

Concept note-4: -Debt to Equity Ratio = Total Liabilities / Shareholders Equity.

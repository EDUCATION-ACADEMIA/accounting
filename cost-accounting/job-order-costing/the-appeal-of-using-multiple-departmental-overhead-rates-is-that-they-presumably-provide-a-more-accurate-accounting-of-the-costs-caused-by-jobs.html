JOB ORDER COSTING

DOWNLOAD FREE PDF BOOK (USEFUL FOR TEACHERS/INSTRUCTORS/COACHING INSTITUTES)

The appeal of using multiple departmental overhead rates is that they presumably provide a more accurate accounting of the costs caused by jobs.

(A) ** True

(B) False

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Answer and Explanation: The statement is true. The departmental overhead rate includes analyzing the expense rate for every department involved in the production phase.

Concept note-2: -Why are multiple overhead rates, rather than a plantwide overhead rate, used in some companies? Some companies used multiple overhead rates because plantwide overhead rate is not always accurate which can lead to overapplication on one product and under application on the other.

Concept note-3: -Traditional Costing uses a single plant wide overhead rate while activity based costing uses a multiple departmental overhead rates. Multiple departmental overhead rates provide a more accurate product cost because it allocates its cost fist to the different activities that are undertaken in producing the product.

Concept note-4: -If your product mix is more complex and customized, you may use multiple overhead rates to allocate costs more accurately. If one department is machine-intensive and another is labor-intensive, for example, multiple rates may be appropriate.

Concept note-5: -Overhead costs in an assembly department may be allocated using direct labor hours. Departmental overhead rates will not correctly assign overhead costs in situations where a company has a range of products and complex overhead costs.

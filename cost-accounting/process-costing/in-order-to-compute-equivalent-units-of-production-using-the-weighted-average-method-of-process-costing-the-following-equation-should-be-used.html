In order to compute equivalent units of production using the Weighted Average method of process costing, the following equation should be used:

(A) Equivalent units of beginning work in process + units completed and transferred out

(B) Equivalent units of beginning work in process + equivalent units of ending work in process

(C) ** Units completed and transferred out + equivalent units of ending work in process

(D) Units completed and transferred out + equivalent units of beginning work in process

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Equivalent units = Number of physical units × Percentage of completion. For direct materials, 3, 000 equivalent units = 5, 000 physical units × 60 percent complete; for direct labor and overhead, 1, 500 equivalent units = 5, 000 physical units × 30 percent complete.

Concept note-2: -Equivalent Units of Production = Units transferred out + Equivalent units in ending work in process inventory − Equivalent units in beginning inventory.

Concept note-3: -In the computation of manufacturing cost per equivalent unit, the weighted-average method of process costing considers: Current costs plus cost of beginning work-in-process inventory.

Concept note-4: -To simply calculate equivalent units, you can multiply the number of physical items by the percentage of the work done on them. For two items that are 50% done, you would have one equivalent unit (2 x 50% = 1). When the items are completely finished, the number of equivalent units is equal to the physical items.

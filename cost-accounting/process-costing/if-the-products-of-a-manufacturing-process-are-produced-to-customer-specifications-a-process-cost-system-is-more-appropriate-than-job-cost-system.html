If the products of a manufacturing process are produced to customer specifications, a process cost system is more appropriate than job cost system.

(A) TRUE

(B) ** FALSE

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -If the products of a manufacturing process are produced to customer specifications, a process cost system is more appropriate than a job order cost system. False. Process manufacturers typically use large machines to process a continuous flow or raw materials into a finished state.

Concept note-2: -Thus, a job order cost system is used for custom jobs when it is easy to determine the cost of materials and labor used for each job. A process cost system is often less expensive to maintain and works best when items are identical and it is difficult to trace the exact cost of materials and labor to the final product.

Concept note-3: -Process costing is a method of costing used mainly in manufacturing where units are continuously mass-produced through one or more processes. Examples of this include the manufacture of erasers, chemicals or processed food.

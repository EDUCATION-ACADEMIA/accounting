In the month of June, a department had 20, 000 units in beginning work in process that were 70% complete. During June, 90, 000 units were transferred into production from another department. At the end of June there were 10, 000 units in ending work in process that were 40% complete. Materials are added at the beginning of the process, while conversion costs are incurred uniformly throughout the process. How many units were transferred out of the process in June?

(A) 90, 000 units.

(B) 80, 000 units.

(C) ** 100, 000 units.

(D) 110, 000 units.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -If the units in the beginning Work-in-Process Inventory are greater than the units in the ending Work-in-Process Inventory, then the units transferred out are: more than the units started during the period.

Concept note-2: -Charley Companyâ€™s Assembly Department has materials cost at $2 per unit and conversion cost at $4 per unit. There are 20, 000 units in ending work in process, all of which are 70% complete as to conversion costs and 100% complete as to materials.

Concept note-3: -The equivalent units of production is the sum of units completed and transferred out and the equivalent units of ending work in process inventory (5, 000 units X 60%) or 20, 000 + 3, 000 = 23, 000 units. Equivalent units of production measure the amount of work that should be completed during a period.

Concept note-4: -An equivalent unit of conversion costs is equal to the amount of conversion costs required to: start and complete a unit. In the computation of the manufacturing cost per equivalent unit, the weighted-average method of process costing considers: current costs plus cost of beginning Work-in-Process Inventory.

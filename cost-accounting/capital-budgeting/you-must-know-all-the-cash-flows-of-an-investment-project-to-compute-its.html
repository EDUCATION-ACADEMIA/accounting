You must know all the cash flows of an investment project to compute its

(A) NPV, IRR, PI and discount payback period

(B) ** NPV, IRR, PI, payback period and discount payback period

(C) NPV, IRR, PI

(D) NPV, accounting rate of return, IRR, PI

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Whereas ROI and NPV only take into account the cash flows of a project, IRR takes into account the timing of those cash flows.

Concept note-2: -To calculate the payback period you can use the mathematical formula: Payback Period = Initial investment / Cash flow per year For example, you have invested Rs 1, 00, 000 with an annual payback of Rs 20, 000. Payback Period = 1, 00, 000/20, 000 = 5 years.

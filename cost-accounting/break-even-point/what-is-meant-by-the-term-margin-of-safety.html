What is meant by the term “margin of safety”?

(A) ** The amount by which the actual output of a business is greater than its break-even output.

(B) The amount of safety involved in producing enough sales revenue to cover total costs

(C) The amount by which the break-even quantity of a business is greater than its actual output.

(D) The amount of sales needed to cover all the costs of the business.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -What is Margin of Safety? The margin of safety is the difference between the amount of expected profitability and the break-even point. The margin of safety formula is equal to current sales minus the breakeven point, divided by current sales.

Concept note-2: -The margin of safety is the difference between actual sales and break-even sales, while the degree of operating leverage (DOL) shows how a company’s operating income changes after a percentage change in its sales.

Concept note-3: -Definition of Margin of Safety In break-even analysis, the term margin of safety indicates the amount of sales that are above the break-even point. In other words, the margin of safety indicates the amount by which a company’s sales could decrease before the company will have no profit.

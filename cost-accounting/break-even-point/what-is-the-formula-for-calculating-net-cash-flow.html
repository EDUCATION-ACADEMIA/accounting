What is the ‘formula’ for calculating Net Cash Flow?

(A) Total receipts + Total Payments

(B) Opening balance + Net cash flow

(C) Total payments-Total receipts

(D) ** Total receipts-Total payments

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -To calculate net cash flow, simply subtract the total cash outflow by the total cash inflow.

Concept note-2: -In order to calculate net cash, you must first add up all cash (not credit) receipts for a period. This amount is often referred to as “gross cash.” Once totaled, cash outflows paid out for obligations and liabilities are deducted from gross cash; the difference is net cash.

Concept note-3: -What is net cash flow? Net cash flow is a profitability metric that represents the amount of money produced or lost by a business during a given period. Usually, you can calculate net cash flow by working out the difference between your business’s cash inflows and cash outflows.

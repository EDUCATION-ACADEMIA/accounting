When factory overhead cost is applied to a job, the work in process account is debited.

(A) ** TRUE

(B) FALSE

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -False. It is false to say that actual manufacturing overhead costs are charged directly to the Work in Process account as the costs are incurred. Actual manufacturing overhead costs are the sum of all indirect materials and labor incurred during the production process of the companyâ€™s offered product.

Concept note-2: -The overhead account is debited for the actual overhead costs as incurred. The overhead account is credited for the overhead costs applied to production in the work-in-process account.

Concept note-3: -As the overhead costs are actually incurred, the Factory Overhead account is debited, and logically offsetting accounts are credited.

Concept note-4: -When indirect labor is applied to a job in process, the manufacturing overhead account is debited. When indirect labor is recorded for a job in process, the work in process account is debited. Overapplied factory overhead that is material in amount is closed to cost of goods sold at year end.

Concept note-5: -A work-in-progress (WIP) is the cost of unfinished goods in the manufacturing process including labor, raw materials, and overhead. WIPs are considered to be a current asset on the balance sheet.

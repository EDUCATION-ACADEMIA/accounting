If the time saved is less than 50 % of the standard time, then the wages under Rowan & Halsey Premium plan on comparison gives

(A) ** More wages to workers under Rowan plan than Halsey plan

(B) More wages to workers under Halsey plan than Rowan plan

(C) Equal wages under two plans

(D) None of the above

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Comparing Rowan plan and Halsey plan, it is seen that when the time saved is less than 50% of the standard time, Rowan plan allows more wages to a worker than Halsey plan.

Concept note-2: -In the Halsey Plan, bonus is usually set at 50% of the time saved. It does not serve as a strong incentive. On the other hand under the Rowan Plan, bonus is that proportion of the wages of the time taken which the time saved bears to the standard time. It serves as a strong incentive for increasing the efficiency.

Concept note-4: -A worker is entitled to get 50% of the wages of the time saved by him/her to complete the job against the time allowed to him/her to do the job under Halsey Premium Plan.

Concept note-5: -Under Halsey plan, bonus is given for 50% of the time saved at the hourly rate. But under the Rowan plan, bonus is given for that portion of the time taken which time saved bears to the standard time at the hourly rate . 4.

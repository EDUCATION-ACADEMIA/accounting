Purchasing, also known as procurement, is the buying of all the materials needed by the organization.

(A) ** True

(B) False

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Answer and Explanation: The above statement is True.

Concept note-2: -Although often used interchangeably, purchasing and procurement are not the same . Purchasing is a part of a broader procurement function and has different methods and goals. Apart from purchasing, procurement also comprises need identification, supplier, and contract management.

Concept note-3: -The terms procurement, purchasing, sourcing and supply chain are often used interchangeably. However, there are important distinctions between them.

Concept note-4: -Purchasing is the process a business or organization uses to acquire goods or services to accomplish its goals. Although there are several organizations that attempt to set standards in the purchasing process, processes can vary greatly between organizations.

Concept note-5: -What is Purchasing? Purchasing is the organized acquisition of goods and services on behalf of the buying entity. Purchasing activities are needed to ensure that needed items are obtained in a timely manner and at a reasonable cost.

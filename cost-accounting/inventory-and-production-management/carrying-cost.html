Carrying cost

(A) Refers to the number of times a company’s inventory is sold and replaced over a certain period of time

(B) Consist of the continual day to day up keep and counting of additions and removals of material, work in process products and the COGS on a daily basis

(C) ** Refer to the costs of maintaining inventory in a company’s warehouse includes costs such as rent, utilities, insurance, taxes, employee costs, etc.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Carrying cost is the amount that a business spends on holding inventory over a period of time. It is the cost of owning, storing, and keeping the items in stock.

Concept note-2: -Inventory carrying cost is the total of all expenses related to storing unsold goods. The total includes intangibles like depreciation and lost opportunity cost as well as warehousing costs. A business’ inventory carrying costs will generally total about 20% to 30% of its total inventory value.

Concept note-3: -Inventory costs are the costs related to storing and maintaining a business’ inventory over a certain period of time.

Concept note-4: -Carrying costs are the various costs a business pays for holding inventory in stock. Examples of carrying costs include warehouse storage fees, taxes, insurance, employee costs, and opportunity costs.

Who would usually make strategic decisions within an organisation?

(A) Shift Supervisor

(B) ** CEO or Managing Director

(C) Middle Management

(D) The Janitor

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A chief executive officer (CEO) and managing director (MD) are high-level professionals who make important decisions to facilitate organisational growth. While a CEO makes strategic decisions and represents the organisation publically, an MD oversees its daily operations and departmental functioning.

Concept note-2: -Leaders and board members execute strategic planning by tying it to their organizationâ€™s vision. Managers, individual contributors, and stakeholders also play pivotal roles in decision-making as businesses strive to increase employee engagement.

Concept note-3: -Strategic decisions are made by the top level management and by the strategists whereas the operational decisions are made by the managers at lower levels. Strategic decisions are related to the contribution to the organizational objectives and goals significantly.

Concept note-4: -The CEO provides data regarding external environment, to senior mangers, guides and helps them in formulating, implementing and evaluating and reformulating strategies at strategic business units are based on the corporate strategies.

Concept note-5: -In the organization, managers at all the levels in the organization take decisions for the achievement of the organizational objectives. Decision making is one of the most important functions of the managers in the organization. It is central managerial activity.

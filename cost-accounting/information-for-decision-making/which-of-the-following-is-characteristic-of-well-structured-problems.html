Which of the following is characteristic of well-structured problems?

(A) They are unusual, never encountered before.

(B) They are complex, high degree of uncertainty.

(C) ** The existing state and desired state are understood.

(D) They often entail high risk.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Well-structured problems are those in which the initial state, goal state, and constraints are clearly defined. Solving WSPs requires procedural knowledge that follows a completely defined and step-by-step, or rote procedure.

Concept note-2: -Examples of well-structured problems (King & Kitchner, 1994, 11) include: Converting a unit of measure between its English and metric equivalents, Solving for X in an algebraic equation, and Calculating the trajectory of a rocketâ€™s flight.

Concept note-3: -Well-structured problems do not require considering alternative arguments, seeking out new evidence, or evaluating the reliability of data and sources of information. Ill-structured problems, on the other hand, are problems about which reasonable people reasonably disagree.

Concept note-4: -In decision making, a problem can be defined as a discrepancy between what exists and what the problem solver desires to exist.

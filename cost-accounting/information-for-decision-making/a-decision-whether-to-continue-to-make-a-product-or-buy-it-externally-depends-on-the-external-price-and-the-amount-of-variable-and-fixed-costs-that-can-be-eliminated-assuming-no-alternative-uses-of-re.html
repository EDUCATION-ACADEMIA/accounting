DOWNLOAD FREE PDF BOOK (USEFUL FOR TEACHERS/INSTRUCTORS/COACHING INSTITUTES)

A decision whether to continue to make a product or buy it externally, depends on the external price and the amount of variable and fixed costs that can be eliminated assuming no alternative uses of resources.

(A) ** true

(B) false

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A decision whether to continue to make a product or buy it externally, depends on the external price and the amount of variable and fixed costs that can be eliminated assuming no alternative uses of resources. An opportunity cost is the potential benefit obtained by using resources in an alternative course of action.

Concept note-2: -A make-or-buy decision is an act of choosing between manufacturing a product in-house or purchasing it from an external supplier.

Concept note-3: -The sell or process further decision is the choice of selling a product now or processing it further to earn additional revenue. This choice is based on an incremental analysis of whether the additional revenues to be gained will exceed the additional costs to be incurred as part of the additional processing work.

Concept note-4: -Incremental costs are relevant in making short-term decisions or choosing between two alternatives, such as whether to accept a special order.

Concept note-5: -Hence, the cost of production is considered for ‘make or buy’ decision.

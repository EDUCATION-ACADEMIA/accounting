Cognitive dissonance is part of:

(A) ** Evaluation of Alternatives and Purchase

(B) Postpurchase behavior

(C) Need recognition

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Cognitive dissonance is a mental conflict that occurs when your beliefs don’t line up with your actions. It’s an uncomfortable state of mind when someone has contradictory values, attitudes, or perspectives about the same thing.

Concept note-2: -A post-purchase dissonance is a form of cognitive dissonance studied in consumer behavior. This form of purchase regret occurs when a customer prizes a specific product and completes a purchase but then experiences dissatisfaction because of the product or service quality.

Concept note-3: -The evaluation of alternatives often involves consumers drawing on their evoke, inept, and insert sets to help them in the decision making process. The brands and products that consumers compare-their evoked set – represent the alternatives being considered by consumers during the problem-solving process.

Concept note-4: -Stage 5. Whether it is or isn’t, you will probably experience cognitive dissonance. Typically, dissonance occurs when a buyer is questioning their decision. Consumers are more likely to experience dissonance with products that are relatively expensive and that are purchased infrequently.

Which of the following steps in the management decision-making process does not generally involve the managerial accountant?

(A) Determine possible courses of action

(B) Prepare internal reports that review the impact of decisions

(C) ** Make the appropriate decision based on relevant data

(D) None of these answer choices are correct.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Making appropriate decisions based on relevant data is the task of executive-level managers; it does not involve managerial accountants.

Concept note-2: -Hence, we conclude that changing the desired outcome is NOT one of the steps involved in the decision-making process.

Concept note-3: -Three of the steps in managementâ€™s decision-making process are: (1) Review results of decision, (2) Identify the problem, (3) make the decision.

Concept note-4: -The correct answer is a. Evaluate possible courses of action, make decision. The management decision-making process should be conducted in the following order: Evaluate all possible courses of action.

The style of a manager who has a deep concern for employees as individuals, likes to talk to people one on one and understand their feelings

(A) directive style

(B) analytical sytle

(C) conceptual style

(D) ** behavioral style

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Behavioral style Managers with a deep concern for others as individuals often adopt a behavioral style. These managers like to talk to people one on one, understand their feelings about the problem, and consider the effect of a given decision on them.

Concept note-2: -Autocratic management style No matter the question, the autocratic leader has the answer. The tagline for this style is, “just do as I say.” Autocratic leaders rely on strict rules, policies, and procedures to govern the workplace. They make all the decisions with little input from team members.

Concept note-3: -The model proposes that the Team leadership style, which displays a high degree of concern for both production and people, may boost employee productivity.

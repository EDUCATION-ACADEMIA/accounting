INFORMATION FOR DECISION MAKING

[SOURCES]
In incremental analysis, total variable costs will always change under alternative courses of action, and total fixed costs will always remain constant.

(A) true

(B) ** false

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -In incremental analysis, total fixed costs will always remain constant under alternative courses of action. A special one-time order should not be accepted if the unit sales price is less than the unit variable cost. A company should never accept an order for its product at less than its regular sales price.

Concept note-2: -Incremental analysis is a true cost comparison. It does not account for sunk costs or previous costs associated with a product, employee or project. Relevant costs covered in an incremental analysis might include: Variable costs: These costs might change from option to option.

Concept note-3: -Variable costs will change under alternative courses of action, but fixed costs will not. When using incremental analysis, some costs will always change under alternative courses of action, but revenues will not. Costs that are the same under all alternative courses of action sometimes affect the decision.

Concept note-4: -In incremental analysis fixed costs may not change under alternative courses of action, while variable costs may change. The relevant data to consider in accepting an order at a special price are the additional manufacturing costs incurred and expected revenues.

Concept note-5: -In incremental analysis, only fixed costs are relevant. costs are not relevant if they change between alternatives. only variable costs are relevant.

DOWNLOAD FREE PDF BOOK (USEFUL FOR TEACHERS/INSTRUCTORS/COACHING INSTITUTES)

A person with good decision-making skills:

(A) waits until the problem has been defined by others.

(B) ** evaluates all the information before making the decision.

(C) always makes the right decision.

(D) makes decisions based on feelings.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A good decision-maker chooses actions that give the best outcome for themselves and others. They enter into the decision-making process with an open mind and do not let their own biases sway them. They make decisions rationally, after researching alternatives and understanding the consequences.

Concept note-2: -Decisions are made explicitly whenever one consciously combines beliefs and values in order to choose a course of action. They are made implicitly whenever one relies on a ritualized response (habit, tradition) to cope with a choice between options.

Concept note-3: -Reasoning. Reasoning is one of the main skills needed to be effective at decision-making. Make sure you take into account all the benefits and disadvantages of every possible solution before you make a selection.
